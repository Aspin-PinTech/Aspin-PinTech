Understanding Your GPA

Why is GPA Important?

Your Grade Point Average (GPA) is a critical metric used by educational institutions and employers to assess your academic performance. It provides a standardized way to compare students' achievements across different schools and courses. A strong GPA can open doors to scholarships, graduate programs, and competitive job opportunities.

How is it Calculated?

The GPA is calculated by converting your letter grades into numerical points (using the scale below), multiplying those points by the number of credits for each course, and then dividing the total grade points by the total number of credits attempted.

Formula: Total Grade Points / Total Credits = GPA
