Navigating the UK University System

Key institutions and processes for prospective students

UCAS Application

The Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS) is the centralized service that students use to apply to UK universities.

  • Most undergraduate applications are made through UCAS.
  • You'll write a personal statement to showcase your suitability.
  • Key deadlines are typically in October (for Oxbridge, Medicine) and January.

University Groups

UK universities are often part of groups with shared characteristics.

  • The Russell Group: Represents 24 leading UK universities committed to research.
  • University Alliance: A group of universities with a focus on professional and technical education.
  • Understanding these can help you choose a university that fits your goals.

Understanding UK Degree Classifications

How UK degrees are graded and what it means

Degree Honours

Unlike the GPA system, UK undergraduate degrees are typically classified with honours. This is the standard grading system you will encounter.

  • First-Class Honours (1st): Typically 70% or higher. The highest level of achievement.
  • Upper Second-Class Honours (2:1): Typically 60-69%. A common requirement for postgraduate study and graduate jobs.
  • Lower Second-Class Honours (2:2): Typically 50-59%.
  • Third-Class Honours (3rd): Typically 40-49%.

Note: Our GPA Calculator is based on the US system and is not applicable for UK degree classifications.

UK Student Visas

Essential information for international students

Student Visa (formerly Tier 4)

If you are an international student aged 16 or over, you will likely need a Student Visa to study in the UK.

  • You must have an unconditional offer from a licensed student sponsor.
  • You need to prove you have enough money to support yourself and pay for your course.
  • You must be able to speak, read, write and understand English.

Working While Studying

Many international students are allowed to work part-time during their studies.

  • Degree-level students can typically work up to 20 hours per week during term time.
  • You can usually work full-time during official vacation periods.
  • Always check the specific conditions printed on your visa vignette or BRP.
